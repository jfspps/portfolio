Software portfolio

I have always had a lifelong interest in solving technical problems and initially pursued a career as a research Chemist. Part of my time was spent teaching others. I then moved into education to gain more experience of teaching Chemistry, learning more about how to explain ideas to others and appreciate different viewpoints.

After I achieved my goals, I decided to pursue my growing interest in computing and become a software developer. What I enjoy most about programming are the small, granular changes made to eventually solve some aspect of a project. I particularly look forward to working in a field which changes quickly and to touch on other industries by helping others solve problems. I have been learning to write (mostly) in Java 11 and feel quite comfortable writing code for extended periods. My projects were predominantly developed on a Linux platform.

Currently, I am looking for opportunities to work as a professional Java developer and apply my background to the healthcare, manufacturing and R&D industries in the UK. Looking further ahead, I am also interested in gaining experience with other software technologies and languages.

Student Record Management

Student record management (SRM) is my second Java project and first venture with Spring MVC. In some schools, teachers and support staff still communicate students' results by exchanging spreadsheets. I wanted to couple my teaching experience with software development and design a simple platform, SRM, which enables teaching staff to record and share student results.

Technologies

  • Java 11
  • Project Lombok
  • MySQL 8
  • Spring MVC 4, Spring Security 5
  • Thymeleaf 3
  • JUnit 5
